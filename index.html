About Me


Passionate. Persistent. Perceptive.


Hi! My name is Justin D'Souza and I am a freshman Computer Engineering student at the University of Waterloo with a strong passion for physics and programming.


Although I was born and raised in Canada, I've lived in Hong Kong, India, and the Philippines; this has enabled me to appreciate cultural differences, attain conversational fluency in Mandarin Chinese, and broaden my outlook on life.


In terms of my technical ability, I have intermediate knowledge of C/C++ and HTML5/CSS3, and I am currently working in Java and VHDL. On top of this, I love to constantly work on personal projects to improve and broaden my programming skill-set. All of the languages I've learned were self-taught, and I believe that the passion and curiousity I have for coding will translate into my success in industry.


Outside of coding, I enjoy playing chess competitively at the national level: my greatest achievement to date has been placing 2nd at the 2016 Canadian University Chess Championships. I also play the clarinet and piano recreationally, and I've completed both my Grade 8 Clarinet and Grade 8 Piano practical exams at the Royal Conservatory of Music with Honours.

Chess and Music


Chess and Music are two of my strongest passions. I've competed in countless chess tournaments at the city, provincial and national levels. I currently represent the University of Waterloo as a top member of their chess team.


With regards to music, I've earned certifcations in the piano, the clarinet and voice with the Royal Conservatory of Music, and I've also competed in Canada's prestigious Kiwanis Music Festivals. I've recently taken up playing the alto-saxophone, and I try to take time each day out of my busy schedule to practice whatever study or piece I'm working on.


Some of my chess achievements include:


1. Second Place, 2016 Canadian University Chess Championships (January 2016 in Kingston, ON)

2. First Place, Toronto Labour Day Chess Tournament (September 2014 in Toronto, ON)

3. Top 10, 2014 Canadian Youth Chess Championships (July 2014 in Montreal, ON)

4. Second Place, Toronto Labour Day Chess Tournament (September 2013 in Toronto, ON)


Some of my music achievements include:


1. Honours, Grade 8 Clarinet Examination, The Royal Conservatory of Music (August 2015)

2. First Class Honours with Distinction, Advanced Rudiments Theory, The Royal Conservatory of Music (December 2014)

3. Honours, Grade 8 Piano Practical Examination, The Royal Conservatory of Music (August 2014)

4. First Class Honours, Grade 5 Voice Examination, The Royal Conservatory of Music (June 2012)
